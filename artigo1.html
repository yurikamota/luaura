Is living abroad in your destiny? What the Vedic Astrology Birth Chart can reveal


Discover the main indicators that point to living in another country


Many people around the world have asked themselves, 'What would my life be like if I lived in another country?' The motivations behind this question vary greatly: from a desire for self-discovery to the pursuit of better financial opportunities. The longing for change is a natural part of being human. However, there is something even greater that may either ease or complicate one’s chances of living abroad for many years. With this in mind, we at the Luaura team have selected the key indicators in a Vedic birth chart that reveal whether someone’s destiny lies in distant lands.

To understand this more clearly, it’s important to know the basics about astrological houses and their rulerships. You can find explanations in the article "Rulerships and the Nature of the Planets in Jyotish".

Rahu in the 12th House

Rahu, known as the North Node in Western astrology, represents everything that is foreign and naturally seeks experiences beyond the comfort zone. That’s why, when positioned in the 12th house – the house furthest from the Ascendant – it intensifies the native’s desire for experiences far from their familiar environment.

Ascendant Lord in the 12th House

The Ascendant is determined by the sign that was rising on the horizon at the moment of our birth. It represents our incarnation and marks the beginning of our journey on Earth. The Ascendant speaks of the place we were born, our first steps, and where we feel we belong.

When the lord of the Ascendant is placed in the 12th house, the native has a natural drive to build their life away from their birthplace. For example, if the Ascendant is Taurus, its ruling planet is Venus. So, if Venus is positioned in the 12th house, the native tends to identify with an international lifestyle.

Saturn in the 4th House

In Vedic Astrology, Saturn is known as a malefic planet. When placed in the 4th house, it creates a sense of discomfort in relation to the native’s homeland, often leading to difficulties that encourage the person to seek new horizons elsewhere.

Ascendant Lord in the 9th House

The 9th house is naturally linked to long-distance travel, fortune abroad, and spirituality. When the lord of the Ascendant is found in this house, the individual feels an inner calling to explore the world, often finding purpose and personal growth far from home.

This doesn’t necessarily mean the native will live abroad permanently, but it suggests that at some point in life, they will be drawn to other cultures and faraway places.

Sun or Moon in the 12th House

With this placement, a person is likely to leave their home country, whether by choice or due to fate. When the Sun is in the 12th house, the native feels that their true shine may be found abroad, often seeking deep spirituality and self-discovery far from the distractions of their homeland.

With the Moon in the 12th house, the drive comes from an emotional need to find a “true home”, crossing deep waters in search of distant lands.

Jupiter in the 9th House

This is considered a lucky placement. It's common for people with this position to move abroad for reasons like studying, NGO missions, or other ways of transcending through a higher purpose.

In short, the 9th and 12th houses are the most connected to long journeys and life in a foreign country. One placement alone doesn’t define the whole destiny, but having several planets in these houses makes the desire to explore distant places much stronger.
